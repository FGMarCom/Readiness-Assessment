Mission & Vision Development 0/4

Define your nonprofit's purpose and establish its foundation

▼
Clarify mission statement and community need
Define a clear, concise mission statement that addresses a genuine community need. Ensure alignment with IRS charitable purposes (charitable, educational, religious, etc.) for 501(c)(3) status.
Critical First Step
Conduct needs assessment
Research existing services in your area to avoid duplication. Document the gap your nonprofit will fill and gather data supporting the need for your services.
Create vision statement
Develop an inspiring vision statement that describes the long-term change you hope to achieve. This guides strategic planning and motivates stakeholders.
Define target beneficiaries and service area
Clearly identify who you will serve, their specific needs, and the geographic area of operation. This focus helps with program design and funding applications.
Pro Tip: Your mission statement should pass the "T-shirt test" - is it clear and compelling enough that supporters would proudly wear it? Keep it under 20 words if possible.

Legal Formation & Registration 0/8

Complete all federal and state legal requirements

▼
Choose and reserve nonprofit name
Select a unique name that complies with state naming rules. Check availability with your Secretary of State and consider reserving it. Also secure matching domain name and social media handles.
State Requirement
File Articles of Incorporation with state
Prepare and file Articles of Incorporation with your state (usually Secretary of State). Include IRS-required language: statement of exempt purpose and dissolution clause for 501(c)(3) status.
Legal Requirement Fee Required
Obtain Employer Identification Number (EIN)
Apply for an EIN from the IRS online (free and immediate). Required for bank accounts, tax filings, and hiring employees. Needed even if you won't have employees initially.
IRS Requirement Free
Draft organizational bylaws
Create comprehensive bylaws covering board structure, officer roles, meeting procedures, and amendment processes. Required by IRS for 501(c)(3) application.
IRS Requirement
Apply for 501(c)(3) tax-exempt status
File IRS Form 1023 ($600 fee) or Form 1023-EZ ($275 fee) within 27 months of incorporation. Include Articles, bylaws, financial projections, and program descriptions.
Federal Requirement Fee Required
Register for state tax exemptions
After receiving IRS determination letter, apply for state income tax and sales tax exemptions. Requirements vary by state - some automatic, others require separate applications.
State Specific
Register for charitable solicitation
If fundraising, register with your state (usually Attorney General) before soliciting donations. 40 states require registration. May need to register in multiple states for online fundraising.
Before Fundraising
Obtain necessary licenses and permits
Research and obtain any required local business licenses, zoning permits, or program-specific licenses (e.g., childcare, food service, healthcare).

Form 1023-EZ Eligibility

  • Projected gross receipts ≤ $50,000 annually
  • Total assets ≤ $250,000
  • Not a church, school, or hospital
  • Not a foreign organization
  • Filing fee: $275 (vs. $600 for full Form 1023)

Board Formation & Governance 0/7

Build a strong, engaged board of directors

▼
Recruit initial board of directors
Assemble at least 3 board members (most states' minimum) who are passionate about the mission and bring diverse skills. Consider expertise in finance, programs, fundraising, and governance.
Legal Minimum
Elect board officers
Designate key officers: President/Chair (leads meetings), Treasurer (financial oversight), and Secretary (maintains records). Define roles clearly in bylaws.
Bylaw Requirement
Hold organizational board meeting
Conduct first official meeting to adopt bylaws, elect officers, approve IRS application, authorize bank account, and set fiscal year. Document all decisions in meeting minutes.
Document Everything
Adopt conflict of interest policy
Implement written policy to manage conflicts. IRS strongly recommends for all 501(c)(3)s. Have each board member sign annually and disclose any potential conflicts.
IRS Best Practice
Establish board committees
Create committees based on needs: Finance, Governance, Fundraising, Programs. Even small boards benefit from committee structure to distribute workload.
Create board orientation materials
Develop board handbook with mission, bylaws, policies, financials, and expectations. Provide training on fiduciary duties (care, loyalty, obedience) and nonprofit governance.
Set regular board meeting schedule
Establish meeting frequency (monthly or quarterly typical for new nonprofits). Create annual calendar, set expectations for attendance and participation.
Board Diversity: Aim for diversity in skills, backgrounds, ages, and perspectives. A diverse board makes better decisions and opens more doors for your organization.

Financial Management Setup 0/8

Establish sound financial systems and controls

▼
Open dedicated nonprofit bank account
Open account in organization's name using EIN. Require two signatures for checks above certain amount. Never commingle personal and organizational funds.
Legal Requirement
Set up accounting system
Implement bookkeeping system (QuickBooks Nonprofit, spreadsheets initially). Create chart of accounts separating income sources and expense categories for easy Form 990 reporting.
For IRS Reporting
Create first-year budget
Develop realistic operating budget including all expenses and projected income. Required for IRS Form 1023 (3-year projections). Board must approve budget annually.
IRS Application
Establish internal financial controls
Implement checks and balances: separate check writing/depositing/reconciling duties, require documentation for expenses, board review of statements, spending approval limits.
Best Practice
Set up donor tracking system
Create system to record donations, donor information, and acknowledgments. Can start with spreadsheet but consider CRM as you grow. Essential for proper receipting.
Create gift acknowledgment templates
Develop IRS-compliant donation receipts. Must include: organization name, amount, date, statement that no goods/services provided (or their value), tax-exempt status.
IRS Requirement
Plan for Form 990 filing
Understand annual filing requirements: 990-N (≤$50k receipts), 990-EZ (≤$200k), or full 990. Due May 15 for calendar year. Failure to file 3 years = automatic revocation.
Annual Requirement
Consider financial expertise
Engage bookkeeper or accountant, especially after receiving funds. At minimum, consult CPA for system setup and first Form 990 filing.
Financial Transparency: Remember that Form 990s are public documents. Donors, media, and watchdog groups can access them. Accuracy and transparency build trust.

Program Development & Evaluation 0/6

Design impactful programs with measurable outcomes

▼
Design core programs aligned with mission
Develop specific programs that directly address identified needs. Define target population, services provided, delivery methods, and required resources.
Set measurable program goals
Establish clear objectives with quantifiable outcomes. Example: "Improve reading levels of 50 children by one grade level within one year." Essential for grant applications.
For Funders
Create implementation timeline
Develop realistic timeline for program rollout. Consider pilot phase to test and refine before full launch. Include milestones and resource allocation.
Establish evaluation metrics
Define how you'll measure success: pre/post tests, surveys, attendance tracking, outcome documentation. Build data collection into program operations from the start.
Identify community partners
Research potential collaborators: other nonprofits, schools, government agencies. Partnerships can enhance programs, avoid duplication, and strengthen funding applications.
Develop program budget
Create detailed budget for each program including staff time, materials, overhead allocation. Understanding true program costs is essential for sustainability and grant writing.

Logic Model Components

  • Inputs: Resources needed (staff, volunteers, funds, materials)
  • Activities: What you'll do (workshops, services, events)
  • Outputs: What you'll produce (# served, sessions held)
  • Outcomes: Changes in participants (knowledge, behavior)
  • Impact: Long-term community change

Fundraising Strategy 0/6

Build sustainable revenue streams

▼
Create comprehensive fundraising plan
Map out funding strategy including individual donations, grants, events, corporate sponsorships, and earned income. Set realistic goals for each revenue stream.
Research grant opportunities
Identify foundations, corporate giving programs, and government grants aligned with your mission. Note application deadlines and requirements. Start with smaller, local grants.
Develop donor cultivation strategy
Plan how to identify, engage, and steward individual donors. Start with board members and close supporters. Remember: people give to people they trust.
Set up online donation capability
Enable online giving through your website. Choose platform considering fees, ease of use, and donor data access. Ensure proper security for payment processing.
Modern Essential
Plan signature fundraising event
If appropriate, design annual event that raises funds and awareness. Consider effort vs. return. Events can be great for visibility but labor-intensive.
Establish board giving expectations
Set clear expectations for board member contributions. 100% board giving (at any level) is important for grant applications and demonstrates commitment.
Best Practice
Diversification: Avoid over-reliance on any single funding source. A healthy nonprofit typically has at least 3-5 different revenue streams to ensure stability.

Operations & Infrastructure 0/8

Establish efficient organizational systems

▼
Determine staffing needs
Decide between all-volunteer model or hiring staff. If hiring, create job descriptions, determine fair compensation, and understand employment law compliance.
Set up payroll systems (if hiring)
Register for payroll taxes, workers' compensation, unemployment insurance. Use payroll service to ensure compliance with tax withholding and reporting requirements.
If Applicable
Secure office space or establish remote setup
Determine workspace needs. Many start from home offices. If renting space, factor in utilities, insurance, and ensure zoning compliance.
Obtain necessary equipment and technology
Acquire computers, software, phone system, and program-specific equipment. Explore nonprofit discounts through TechSoup, Microsoft, Google for Nonprofits.
Cost Savings Available
Purchase appropriate insurance coverage
Obtain general liability, directors & officers (D&O), property insurance, and workers' comp if applicable. Consult insurance broker familiar with nonprofits.
Risk Management
Develop personnel policies
Create employee handbook covering work hours, leave policies, code of conduct, anti-discrimination policies. Ensure compliance with federal and state employment laws.
If Hiring
Establish record-keeping systems
Set up organized filing for corporate documents, financial records, donor information, and program data. Implement document retention policy for compliance.
Create volunteer management system
Develop volunteer recruitment, screening, training, and recognition processes. Consider volunteer agreements and background checks if working with vulnerable populations.

Essential Insurance Types

  • General Liability: Covers accidents, injuries at events
  • D&O Insurance: Protects board members' decisions
  • Property: Covers equipment, office contents
  • Workers' Comp: Required with employees in most states
  • Professional Liability: If providing professional services

Marketing & Community Outreach 0/6

Build visibility and engage your community

▼
Develop brand identity
Create professional logo, select colors and fonts, develop brand guidelines. Consistent branding builds recognition and trust. Consider pro bono design help.
Launch professional website
Create website with mission, programs, donation capability, and contact info. Ensure mobile-friendly design. Consider SEO to help people find you.
Modern Essential
Establish social media presence
Create profiles on platforms where your audience is active. Develop content calendar for regular posting. Share stories, updates, and calls to action.
Build email list and newsletter
Start collecting email addresses from supporters. Send regular updates about impact, needs, and opportunities. Use email marketing platform for compliance and analytics.
Create marketing materials
Develop brochure, one-page fact sheet, and presentation template. Ensure consistent messaging about mission, programs, and impact across all materials.
Plan community engagement strategy
Identify speaking opportunities, community events, and networking groups. Build relationships with local media. Consider hosting open house or launch event.
Storytelling Power: People connect with stories, not statistics. Collect and share stories of lives changed by your work (with permission). One powerful story can inspire more support than pages of data.

Ongoing Compliance & Reporting 0/7

Maintain legal status and accountability

▼
File annual Form 990 with IRS
File appropriate version by May 15 annually. Remember: failure to file for 3 consecutive years results in automatic revocation of tax-exempt status.
Annual Requirement
Submit state annual reports
File required state corporate annual/biennial reports to maintain good standing. Update officer and address information. Pay required fees on time.
State Requirement
Renew charitable solicitation registration
Renew state fundraising registrations annually in all states where you solicit donations. Include Form 990 and any required financial reports.
Maintain public support test
After 5 years, ensure you meet IRS public support requirements to remain a public charity (vs. private foundation). Generally need 1/3 of support from public sources.
Follow lobbying limitations
Understand that 501(c)(3)s can do limited lobbying but no political campaign activity. Consider filing 501(h) election if you plan to lobby regularly.
Know the Rules
Conduct annual board review
Review and update bylaws, policies, and board composition annually. Ensure continued alignment with mission and compliance with all regulations.
Consider independent audit
Plan for financial audit when required by state law (often at $500k+ revenue) or requested by major funders. Builds credibility and identifies control improvements.
Compliance Calendar: Create an annual compliance calendar with all filing deadlines. Assign responsibility and set reminders 30 days before each deadline to avoid penalties.
